Always the Next Best Thing

The original iPad was an iPhone gone IMAX and the first successful tablet the world had ever seen. On January 27, 2010, Steve Jobs took the state at an Apple special event to give one of the most important keynote speeches of his life and in the history of consumer electronics. The Mac had been introduced decades before, and the iPhone was only a few years old. Jobs made the case that there was room for a new category of device in between them. The original iPad had a 9.7 inch screen and no camera! By April of 2011, Apple had discontinued the original iPad and the total sales had reached over 15 million units. That was more than the original iPhone sold in it's first year and more than every other tablet combined had ever sold

The iPad 2 was made thinner, lighter, and faster. On March 2, 2011, Steve Jobs returned to the keynote stage and came back with a much better idea for the iPad. The iPad2 had the same 9.7 inch screen but was up to twice as fast for CPU and up to 9 times as fast for GPU tasks. It was 33% thinner and weont from 1.5 to 1.33 lbs. There were cameras added, back and front.





The iPad 3 went Retina and LTE. Apple's third generation, 2012 iPad, called simply the New iPad was announced on March 7, 2012. It looks virtually identical to the 2011 iPad 2, but its technology is far more advanced. The cameras did not satisfy users and was not that impressive. Apple discontinued the iPad 3 after only 6 months, replacing it with the iPad 4




The iPad 4 made Retina and LTE even better. The announcement was made on October 23, 2013. While it still looks like the 2011 iPad2, it is has an Apple A6x processor, international LTE networking and a FaceTime HD camera that combine to make it a reasonable upgrade. This iPad was discontinued in the spring of 2013 and replaced by the iPad air. It came back in the spring of 2014 to replace the iPad 2. The iPad mini showed portability could be just as important as power.
